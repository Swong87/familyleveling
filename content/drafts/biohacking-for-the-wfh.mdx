Omega-3 fatty acids — specifically **EPA** and **DHA** — are essential fats that play a critical role in brain structure and function. Your brain is literally built from fat, and omega-3s are one of the main materials.

For WFH parents, omega-3s can support:
- Focus and attention
- Mood regulation
- Reduced mental fatigue
- Lower systemic inflammation
- Cardiovascular health (long-term cognitive protection)

In practical terms, omega-3s help your brain stay **chemically stable under stress**.

### How It’s Commonly Used
- Typical dose: **2 softgels per day with food**
- Best taken with meals that contain fat (improves absorption)
- Consistency matters more than timing

Most people notice subtle effects over **2–4 weeks**, not immediately.

### Warnings & Possible Issues
Omega-3s are generally safe, but not risk-free.

Be aware of:

- **Digestive issues:** Some people experience fishy burps, bloating, or mild nausea.
- **Allergies:** Not suitable for people with fish or shellfish allergies.
- **Blood thinning:** Omega-3s have mild anticoagulant effects.
  - Use caution if you take blood thinners (warfarin, aspirin, etc.).
  - Avoid high doses before surgery unless cleared by a doctor.
- **Overdosing:** Very high doses (over ~3g/day EPA+DHA) may increase bleeding risk.

**Rule of thumb:** Stick to label directions unless supervised by a clinician.

Omega-3s are not stimulants. They’re **structural brain support**, not instant energy.

Magnesium is one of the most underrated nutrients for stressed adults.

It plays a role in:
- Nervous system regulation
- Sleep quality
- Muscle relaxation
- Emotional resilience
- Energy metabolism

If omega-3s support *brain hardware*, magnesium supports the **operating system**.

For WFH parents, magnesium often helps with:
- Feeling less “wired but tired”
- Reducing evening mental agitation
- Improving sleep recovery
- Lowering stress reactivity

### Why This Form Matters
Doctor’s Best uses **chelated magnesium (glycinate + lysinate)**, which:
- Is better absorbed
- Is gentler on the stomach
- Is less likely to cause diarrhea than magnesium oxide

This makes it one of the most practical forms for daily use.

### How It’s Commonly Used
- Typical dose: **100–200 mg in the evening**
- Best taken after dinner or before bed
- Works synergistically with good sleep hygiene

Effects are often noticeable within **days**, especially for sleep and stress.

### Warnings & Possible Issues

Magnesium is safe for most people, but:

- **Digestive effects:** Too much can cause diarrhea or cramping.
- **Upper limit:** Don’t exceed **~350 mg/day from supplements** without medical guidance.
- **Kidney conditions:** People with kidney disease should avoid supplementing without doctor approval.
- **Medication interactions:**
  - Can interfere with absorption of some antibiotics
  - Can affect blood pressure medications

If you feel unusually drowsy, weak, or nauseous — reduce dose.

Magnesium is not a sedative.
It’s a **nervous system stabilizer**.
